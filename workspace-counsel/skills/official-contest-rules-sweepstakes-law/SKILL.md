# SKILL: Official Contest Rules & Sweepstakes Law

## Purpose
Draft legally compliant official rules for paid skill competitions, sweepstakes, and prize contests. Covers federal requirements, state registration mandates (New York, Florida), no-purchase-necessary alternative entry, void-where-prohibited language, and prize notification procedures.

## Risk Level
🟡 Medium — Missing or defective contest rules are a direct FTC and state AG enforcement trigger. New York and Florida require advance registration for large-prize contests. Getting this wrong exposes the platform to injunctions, fines, and prize clawback liability.

---

## Three Types of Promotions — Know the Difference

### 1. Sweepstakes (Chance + Prize, No Consideration)
- Winners selected by random chance
- **No purchase required** — legal requirement to remove "consideration" element
- No skill involved
- Lowest regulatory burden
- FTC guidelines apply; state registration may apply

### 2. Contest (Skill + Prize + Consideration)
- Winners selected based on skill/merit
- Entry fee permitted because skill (not chance) determines outcome
- **Agent Arena / Bouts model**
- Higher regulatory scrutiny than sweepstakes
- Must be genuinely skill-based (not disguised lottery)

### 3. Lottery (Chance + Prize + Consideration)
- All three elements present
- **ILLEGAL** unless operated by state lottery authority
- If your contest has significant chance element + entry fee = lottery = illegal
- Avoid at all costs

## Federal Requirements

### FTC Guidelines for Promotional Marketing
- No deceptive practices in contest advertising (FTC Act § 5)
- Must clearly disclose: odds of winning, entry requirements, prize description
- Cannot require purchase for sweepstakes (would create lottery)
- Must honor stated prize awards
- Must not discriminate in prize awards

### CAN-SPAM / TCPA
- Post-contest follow-up emails require CAN-SPAM compliance
- Contest entry cannot be conditioned on marketing consent
- Notification of winning must include opt-out mechanism

### COPPA
- Cannot collect information from participants under 13
- Age gate must precede any data collection
- If any chance under-13 users could enter: COPPA compliance required in data practices

## State Registration Requirements

### New York (CRITICAL)
**Law**: NY Gen. Bus. Law § 369-e; NY Penal Law § 225
**Registration required when**:
- Prize value exceeds **$5,000** total for a single promotion
- Must register with NY Secretary of State **before** promotion begins

**Registration requirements**:
- Form: NY Contest/Sweepstakes registration form
- Filing fee: $50
- Bond requirement: If prize > $5,000, must post bond equal to prize value OR file affidavit of prize award escrow
- Official rules must be filed with registration
- **Timeline**: File 30 days before promotion begins

**Contact**: NY Secretary of State, One Commerce Plaza, 99 Washington Ave., Albany, NY 12231

**Penalty for non-compliance**: Class A misdemeanor; prize clawback; injunction

### Florida (CRITICAL)
**Law**: Florida Statutes § 849.094 (game promotions)
**Registration required when**:
- Prize value exceeds **$5,000** total
- Must register with Florida Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services **before** promotion begins

**Registration requirements**:
- File: "Game Promotion" registration form
- Fee: $100
- Surety bond: Equal to prize pool OR trust account holding prize funds
- Official rules must be posted
- **Timeline**: File before launch

**Contact**: Florida Dept. of Agriculture and Consumer Services, Division of Consumer Services, Tallahassee, FL

**Penalty**: Injunction, fines, criminal violation

### Other States with Requirements
- **Rhode Island**: Registration if prize > $500 for contests of chance
- **Tennessee**: Annual registration for certain promotional contests
- **Arizona**: Lottery restrictions apply; skill game permits for some structures
- **Most other states**: Official rules + void-where-prohibited language sufficient; no formal registration

## Official Contest Rules — Required Elements

Every paid skill competition must have published Official Rules containing:

### 1. Sponsor/Operator Identification
```
Sponsor: [Company Legal Name], [Address], [State of Incorporation]
```
Must be the actual legal entity operating the contest, not just brand name.

### 2. Eligibility
```
Open to legal residents of the United States (except where prohibited — 
see "Void Where Prohibited" below), 18 years of age or older as of date 
of entry. Employees of Sponsor and their immediate family members are not eligible.
```

**Must include**:
- Age requirement (18+ minimum; 21+ if any state requires)
- Jurisdiction exclusions (list blocked states explicitly — see `blocked-jurisdiction-list` skill)
- Conflict of interest exclusions (employees, contractors, their families)
- Tax ID requirement for winners (if prizes are reportable)

### 3. Contest Period
```
Contest begins: [Date/Time] [Timezone]
Entry deadline: [Date/Time] [Timezone]  
Winner announced: [Date/Time] [Timezone]
```
Must use specific times with timezone. "Until entries close" is insufficient.

### 4. How to Enter
```
Paid entry: Submit [description of submission] at [URL] and pay entry fee of $[X]. 
Limit [X] entries per person.

No-purchase alternative entry (skill contest): [If required — see below]
```

### 5. No-Purchase Necessary (Sweepstakes Only)
For **sweepstakes** (chance-based): Must provide free alternative entry method
```
To enter without purchase, mail a 3x5 index card with your name, address, 
email, and phone to [Address] to receive one free entry. 
Handwritten entries only. One free entry per person per day.
```
**For paid skill competitions**: No-purchase alternative entry is generally NOT required because entry fee is for a skill contest, not a lottery. However, including it reduces regulatory risk in ambiguous jurisdictions.

### 6. Prize Description
```
Grand Prize: [Exact description + value]. Approximate Retail Value (ARV): $[X].
[Second prize, etc.]
Total ARV of all prizes: $[X].
```
**Critical**: ARV must be stated. Cannot claim prize value higher than actual value. If prize is crypto/USDC, state USD value at time of award calculation methodology.

### 7. Winner Determination / Judging Criteria
For skill competitions:
```
Winners determined by [scoring methodology]. Judging criteria: 
[Criterion 1] ([X]% weight), [Criterion 2] ([X]% weight), [Criterion 3] ([X]% weight).
In the event of a tie: [tiebreaker procedure].
Sponsor's decisions are final and binding.
```
**Critical for Agent Arena**: Must specify that AI agent performance is evaluated by defined, objective criteria. Subjective criteria = harder to defend as skill game.

### 8. Odds of Winning
```
Odds of winning depend on the number of eligible entries received and the 
relative skill demonstrated by each entry.
```
For skill games: Odds depend on skill, not chance — this language is appropriate.

### 9. Prize Award Procedures
```
Potential winners will be notified by [email/phone] within [X] days of 
contest close. Potential winner must respond within [X] days of notification 
or prize will be forfeited. 
Winner must complete a W-9 (US) or W-8BEN (international) before prize payment. 
Prizes paid within [X] days of winner verification.
```

### 10. Tax Obligations
```
All federal, state, and local taxes on prizes are the sole responsibility 
of the winner. Sponsor will issue IRS Form 1099-MISC for prizes valued at 
$600 or more in a calendar year. Winners may be required to provide a valid 
Social Security Number or Tax Identification Number as a condition of prize receipt.
```

### 11. General Conditions
```
Sponsor reserves the right to modify, suspend, or terminate this contest 
at any time for any reason. Entries that are fraudulent, mechanically reproduced, 
or submitted through automated means are void. 
By participating, entrants agree to be bound by these Official Rules and 
Sponsor's decisions, which are final.
```

### 12. Dispute Resolution
```
All disputes arising from this Contest shall be resolved by binding 
arbitration under AAA rules, conducted in [State], under the laws of [State]. 
Class action waiver: Entrants waive the right to participate in class action proceedings.
```

### 13. Void Where Prohibited
```
THIS CONTEST IS VOID WHERE PROHIBITED OR RESTRICTED BY LAW. 
Not available to residents of: Washington, Arizona, Louisiana, Montana, Idaho, 
[and any other blocked states per platform's blocked jurisdiction list].
```

### 14. Governing Law
```
These Official Rules shall be governed by the laws of the State of [Iowa/Delaware], 
without regard to conflicts of law principles.
```

### 15. Sponsor Contact / Official Rules Availability
```
For a copy of the Official Rules or winner list, send a self-addressed 
stamped envelope to: [Address] or visit [URL].
```

## For Agent Arena / Bouts Specifically

### What to Include in Contest Rules
1. AI agent submission requirements (format, runtime, allowed libraries)
2. Judging criteria (must be objective and pre-stated — test cases, benchmarks, scoring rubric)
3. Ownership of submitted code (IP assignment or license-back clause)
4. Prohibition on plagiarism / pre-staged solutions
5. Right of Sponsor to audit AI agent submissions
6. Prohibition on agents that exploit judging infrastructure (security requirement)
7. Participant vs. bettor segregation (if wagering layer exists)

### Iowa DIA Registration
Iowa Code § 99B.5(2): Register with Iowa DIA if any single contest's prize > $500
- This is an Iowa-specific requirement Nick must comply with as Iowa operator
- File with Iowa DIA **before** first paid contest regardless of prize size (since prizes will likely exceed $500)

- [ ] Official Rules drafted and reviewed
- [ ] All required elements present (see list above)
- [ ] Blocked jurisdictions listed explicitly
- [ ] NY registration filed (if prize > $5,000)
- [ ] FL registration filed (if prize > $5,000)
- [ ] Iowa DIA registration filed (if prize > $500)
- [ ] Prize funds escrowed or bonded (NY/FL requirement)
- [ ] Official Rules URL live on platform before entries open
- [ ] W-9 collection flow built for winners
- [ ] 1099-MISC issuance process confirmed

## Iowa Angle
- Iowa Code § 99B.5: Skill contests explicitly legal; registration required if prize >$500
- Iowa DIA (Department of Inspections and Appeals): registering Iowa contests; friendly process
- Iowa-based company operating contests: Iowa law governs; Iowa's framework is favorable for skill games
- Iowa Code § 99B.6: Promotional contests (sweepstakes) permitted without license if no purchase required
