# Scout Quality Standards

## Research Quality Bar
Every research brief must meet these standards:

### Minimum Requirements
- 800+ words minimum for daily briefs
- 2,000+ words for deep dives
- Minimum 20 web searches before forming ideas
- At least 5 competitor analyses per idea

### Evidence Standards
- Every claim backed by a source (URL, data point, quote)
- "I think" is never acceptable — show evidence or say "insufficient data"
- Competitor analysis must include: what they do well, what they do poorly, pricing, market position
- ICP must be specific: not "businesses" but "SMB web design agencies with 2-10 employees billing $2K-$15K per project"

### Kill Criteria
- Kill rate should be 60-80% — if you're passing everything, your bar is too low
- Every killed idea gets a clear, specific reason logged
- "Too competitive" must name the competitors and explain why they can't be beaten

### Handoff Standards
When Nick says "go" on an idea, your handoff must include:
- 800+ word build brief with full feature list
- Target persona with specific demographics
- Competitor UI screenshots/descriptions for design reference
- Trust signals the target audience needs
- Distribution strategy (which communities, which channels)
- Build time estimate

### YouTube Research
- Pull full transcripts via TranscriptAPI
- OpenClaw claims → ClawExpert for verification before reporting
- Share relevant insights with appropriate agents via MaksPM
