# Market Analysis Frameworks

## TAM/SAM/SOM Estimation
For every market:
- **TAM** (Total Addressable Market): Every possible customer × revenue
- **SAM** (Serviceable Addressable Market): The reachable portion
- **SOM** (Serviceable Obtainable Market): What you capture in 1-2 years

### Estimation Methods
1. Top-down: Industry reports → segment → geography → niche
2. Bottom-up: # potential customers × average revenue per customer
3. Comparable: Similar companies' revenue as proxy
Always provide BOTH top-down and bottom-up.

## Porter's Five Forces
For competitive analysis:
1. **Competitive rivalry**: How many? How differentiated?
2. **Supplier power**: Platform dependencies?
3. **Buyer power**: Switching cost? Price sensitivity?
4. **Substitution threat**: Alternative solutions?
5. **Entry barriers**: How hard to compete?

## Demand Validation Score
Rate 1-5 on each:
- **Problem severity** (1=nice-to-have, 5=hair-on-fire)
- **Solution gap** (1=well-served, 5=badly underserved)
- **Willingness to pay** (1=no budget, 5=already paying for worse)
- **Market size** (1=tiny, 5=massive)
- **Distribution clarity** (1=no path, 5=obvious channel)
- **Technical feasibility** (1=years, 5=weeks with our stack)
Total /30. >22 = strong. <15 = pass.

## ICP Framework
For every product:
- **Who**: Demographics, role, company size
- **What**: Problem they have
- **Where**: Online hangouts (subreddits, Slack, X, forums)
- **When**: Trigger event that creates the need
- **Why now**: Why solvable now vs before
- **Budget**: Current spend on alternatives

## Research Output Standard
Every brief MUST include:
1. Product summary (what, who)
2. Feature list (must-have vs nice-to-have, prioritized)
3. TAM/SAM/SOM estimate with methodology
4. Competitor analysis (3-5 minimum)
5. ICP definition
6. Demand validation score (/30)
7. UX patterns worth replicating (specific URLs)
8. Technical requirements (auth, payments, real-time)
9. Distribution channels (where to find customers)
10. **Recommendation: GO / MAYBE / PASS** with reasoning
