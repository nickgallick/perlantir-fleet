# Discord Community Strategy

## Target Communities for Developer Products

### AI Agent Communities
- **LangChain Discord** — Largest agent framework community. Share-your-project channels exist.
- **CrewAI Discord** — Multi-agent enthusiasts. Competitive angle resonates.
- **AutoGen/AG2 Discord** — Microsoft-adjacent agent builders.
- **Hugging Face Discord** — Open-weight model community. Weight class angle plays well here.
- **OpenAI Developer Forum/Discord** — API users, builders on GPT models.
- **Anthropic Discord** — Claude-focused builders.

### Competitive Coding / AI Eval
- **Kaggle community** — Competition-minded ML practitioners
- **MLContests Discord** — People who actively seek AI competitions
- **Competitive programming servers** — May cross over to agent competitions

### Developer Tools
- **Vercel Discord** — Next.js builders, deployment community
- **Supabase Discord** — Backend/database community
- **OpenClaw community** — AI agent builders, directly relevant

## Engagement Rules

**Never do:**
- Join a server and immediately post your product link
- DM members with product pitches
- Post the same message across multiple channels
- Use bot accounts for engagement
- Post in off-topic channels

**Always do:**
- Lurk for 1-2 weeks before posting anything self-promotional
- Answer questions in help channels first
- Contribute to technical discussions
- Use the designated share-your-project or showcase channels
- Ask genuine questions about the community's domain

## Engagement Sequence

### Week 1-2: Establish Presence
- Join relevant servers
- Read channel rules and pinned messages
- Answer 3-5 questions in help/discussion channels
- React to and engage with others' posts

### Week 3: Soft Introduction
- In a relevant discussion, mention what you're building
- Frame it as: "I'm working on [X] and ran into [related problem]..."
- Ask for feedback on a specific technical decision
- Share a screenshot or insight, not a link

### Week 4+: Share Your Work
- Post in showcase/share-your-project channels
- Include: what it is, screenshot/video, what feedback you want
- Respond to all comments
- Offer early access or beta invites to engaged members

## Post Format for Showcase Channels

```
🚀 [Product Name] — [One line description]

[2-3 sentences about what it does and why]

[Screenshot or demo GIF]

Looking for feedback on:
- [Specific question 1]
- [Specific question 2]

Try it: [link]
Stack: [brief stack]
```

## Community-Specific Value-Add

For each community, offer something unique:
- **LangChain Discord:** "Here's how to connect your LangChain agent to compete"
- **Hugging Face:** "Local models compete in their own weight class — no API cost disadvantage"
- **Vercel:** "Built with Next.js + Vercel, here's the architecture"
- **Competitive coding:** "Same competitive thrill, but your AI agent does the coding"

## Ongoing Engagement

After launch, maintain presence:
- Share interesting data/results weekly
- Announce new challenges or features in relevant channels
- Continue answering questions (not just about your product)
- Cross-pollinate: share insights from one community in another (with credit)
- Aim for 5:1 ratio of helpful comments to self-promotional posts
