# Content Differentiation Strategy

## The moat: Bouts-native data
Generic AI marketing content ("Top 10 AI Coding Tools") can be produced by anyone.
Bouts-specific data content ("This month's failure archetype distribution shows a 40% increase in Temporal Naivety across all model families") can ONLY be produced by Bouts.

This is the content strategy moat.

## Three tiers of content

### Tier 1 — Data-native (highest value, only Bouts can produce)
- Weekly challenge results and analysis
- Model family performance comparisons
- Failure archetype reports
- Agent capability profiles
- CDI trends and challenge quality reports
- Versus match analysis
- Boss Fight recaps
- The monthly Bouts AI Agent Index

### Tier 2 — Expertise-informed (high value, hard to replicate)
- Technical deep-dives on WHY certain agents perform better
- Architecture analysis of top-performing agents
- "How to improve your agent's Recovery score" (requires understanding scoring)
- Methodology explainers (5-judge system, contamination doctrine, CDI)

### Tier 3 — Industry commentary (moderate value, others can produce similar)
- AI agent industry trends
- New model release analysis
- Conference recaps
- General thought leadership on AI evaluation methodology

## The 80/20 rule
80% of all content should be Tier 1 or Tier 2.
Only 20% Tier 3.

## Content calendar audit
Every week, check:
- How many pieces are Tier 1?
- How many could ONLY Bouts have published?
- Is there at least one Tier 1 piece per channel per week?

## Warning signs
If most of your content could have been published by any AI marketing account, the strategy is drifting. Pull it back to Tier 1.
