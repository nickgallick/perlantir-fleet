# LinkedIn Post Formats

## Hook Engineering

The first line determines everything. LinkedIn truncates to ~210 characters before "...see more". Your hook must earn that click.

### Hook Formulas:

**Curiosity Gap:**
"I almost shut down my AI platform last week."
"The best AI benchmark in the world is broken. Here's proof."

**Bold Claim:**
"ELO ratings are a better evaluation system for AI than any benchmark."
"Your AI agent is only as good as its worst competition."

**Specific Story:**
"Tuesday, 11 PM. 47 AI agents are competing simultaneously. I'm watching from my phone."
"6 months ago I had zero users. Yesterday, 200 agents competed on my platform."

**Counter-Intuitive:**
"We made AI agents WORSE at coding. On purpose. Here's why."
"The most popular weight class on Agent Arena isn't the biggest models."

**Data Lead:**
"After 500 AI agent battles, here's the one thing that predicts who wins."
"73% of AI benchmarks don't measure what they claim to. We built something different."

### Rules:
- Write 3 hook variants per post, pick the best
- Never reveal the payoff in the hook — create the gap
- Test: would YOU stop scrolling for this?

## Post Types

### Story Post (Highest Engagement)
```
[Hook — specific moment in time]

[1-2 sentences setting the scene]

[The tension/challenge — what went wrong or what was at stake]

[The turn — what you did, decided, or discovered]

[The resolution — what happened]

[The transferable insight — what others can learn]

[CTA question — invite a reply]
```

**Arena example:**
```
Tuesday, 11 PM. I'm watching 47 AI agents compete in real-time.

A fine-tuned Llama 3 — running on a laptop — is beating GPT-5.

Not in a cherry-picked benchmark. In a timed, blind coding challenge with live scoring.

We built Agent Arena because static benchmarks don't tell you how models perform under pressure.

Weight classes keep it fair. ELO keeps it honest.

After 500 battles, the leaderboard tells a very different story than the benchmark papers.

What's the most surprising AI evaluation result you've seen?
```

### Expertise Post
```
[Hook — what most people get wrong]

[The common belief and why it's wrong]

[Your mental model / framework]

[Evidence — specific example or data]

[Actionable takeaway]

[CTA]
```

### Opinion Post
```
[Hook — the hot take]

[State the position clearly]

[Acknowledge the counterargument — "I know what you're thinking..."]

[Defend with evidence]

[Why this matters for the reader]

[Invite the debate]
```

### Data Post
```
[Hook — the surprising number]

[Context — where this data came from]

[2-3 key findings]

[What it means / implications]

[CTA]
```

## Carousel Format

Upload as PDF for native rendering. 8-12 slides.

```
Slide 1: HOOK (same rules as post hooks — this IS the post)
Slide 2: The problem / context
Slides 3-7: One insight per slide, max 15 words + visual
Slide 8: The big reveal / key takeaway
Slide 9: CTA — "Follow for more [topic]. Save this for reference."
```

**Arena carousel ideas:**
- "6 Weight Classes of AI: Where Does Your Model Fit?"
- "What 500 AI Agent Battles Taught Us"
- "How ELO Ratings Work (And Why AI Needs Them)"
- "The Biggest AI Benchmark Myths — Busted"

## Formatting Rules

- One idea per paragraph. 1-3 lines max.
- Use line breaks generously — white space is engagement on LinkedIn.
- Break the post at a tension point to force "...see more" — the payoff must be below the fold.
- No emojis in professional content (1-2 max if using for bullet structure).
- Bold (**text**) sparingly for emphasis — LinkedIn supports it.
- Lists and numbered points work well for scanners.

## CTA Patterns

**Good:** "What's been your experience with [topic]?" (invites reply)
**Good:** "Agree or disagree? I'm curious." (invites debate)
**Good:** "Save this if you're building [related thing]." (drives saves)
**Bad:** "Like if you agree!" (empty engagement bait)
**Bad:** "Check out [link]!" (suppressed by algorithm)
**OK:** "Link to [thing] in first comment." (accepted workaround)
