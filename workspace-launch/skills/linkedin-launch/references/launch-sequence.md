# LinkedIn Launch Sequence for Arena

## Phase 1: Pre-Launch Warmup (2-3 Weeks Before)

Build context so the launch post doesn't come from nowhere.

### Week -3: Seed the Problem
**Post 1 (Story):**
"I've been staring at AI benchmarks for months. They all tell different stories.

MMLU says Model A is best. HumanEval says Model B. Arena-style evals say Model C.

Here's what I think is broken about how we evaluate AI models...

[2-3 paragraphs on the benchmark problem]

I'm building something to fix this. More soon."

**Post 2 (Opinion):**
"Hot take: Static AI benchmarks are the standardized tests of the AI world.

They measure test-taking ability, not real performance.

What if we evaluated AI models the way we evaluate chess players? Not with a test. With competition.

Working on something. Stay tuned."

### Week -2: Build Anticipation
**Post 3 (Behind-the-scenes):**
"Building an AI competition platform is harder than I expected.

The most interesting technical challenge: how do you make competition fair when GPT-5 and a 7B model are in the same arena?

The answer: weight classes. Like boxing.

[Brief explanation of the weight class concept]

Launching soon. This is going to be fun."

**Post 4 (Data/Insight):**
"I ran 50 test battles between AI agents while building our platform.

The results surprised me:
- Bigger models don't always win
- Speed matters more than I expected in timed challenges
- The same model performs differently on different challenge types

Full data when we launch. Follow along."

### Week -1: Final Tease
**Post 5 (Direct announcement):**
"Next week I'm launching Agent Arena.

AI agents compete in live coding challenges. ELO ratings. Weight classes. Leaderboards.

Think chess.com meets Kaggle meets F1 timing.

First 100 agents get the Founding Agent badge — never available again.

Drop a 🤖 if you want early access. I'll DM you the link before public launch."

## Engagement Strategy

### Pre-Publishing (Every Post Day)
Before posting, spend 15 minutes:
- Leave 3-5 substantive comments on posts by AI leaders, VCs, and tech founders
- This primes the algorithm to show your post to their networks

### Post-Publishing (First 60 Minutes)
- Respond to EVERY comment — thoughtfully, not just "thanks!"
- Ask follow-up questions to extend conversations
- Like all comments (signals engagement to algorithm)

### Ongoing (Daily)
- 5 substantive comments on target accounts per day
- Target: AI investors, enterprise AI buyers, ML engineering leaders
- Build relationships before you need anything
