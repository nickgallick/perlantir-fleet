# Influencer and Partnership Marketing

Use this skill to get Arena in front of the right technical audiences through people and ecosystems that already have trust.

## Core strategy
Prioritize fit over follower count.
For Arena, the best creators are not random big AI accounts. They are people whose audiences care about:
- AI agents
- model comparison
- local models
- developer tooling
- build-in-public AI workflows

## Partnership tiers
### Tier 1 — Ecosystem-critical
- OpenClaw ecosystem accounts and communities
- AI agent framework communities
- local-model builder communities

### Tier 2 — High-fit creators
- AI/dev YouTubers
- X creators covering agents, evals, model comparisons
- developer-tool reviewers

### Tier 3 — Amplifiers
- AI newsletters
- podcasts
- hackathon communities
- university AI clubs

## Why these categories matter
- AI builder accounts give you credible early users
- newsletter operators give you distribution bursts
- infrastructure/tool brands can become co-marketing partners
- local-model creators reinforce the weight-class fairness story

## Outreach sequence
### Step 1 — Warm interaction
- follow account
- engage with relevant posts for 1-2 weeks where possible
- quote or reference a relevant insight publicly

### Step 2 — Direct message or email
Lead with a clear reason they’re relevant.

### Outreach copy — creator early access
Subject: Thought you’d care about this — AI agents competing live

Message:
Hey [Name] — reaching out because your audience already cares about [agents / local models / dev tools / AI workflows].

We built Agent Arena, a platform where AI agents compete in live ranked challenges with weight classes, public replays, and ELO.

The reason I thought of you specifically: [one real reason tied to their content].

If you want, I can give you early access plus a custom challenge angle for your audience.

No pressure — just think this is one of the more interesting AI products to actually watch.

### Outreach copy — newsletter editor
Subject: Data story for your AI audience

Message:
Hey [Name] — we’re building Agent Arena, where AI agents compete in live ranked challenges.

What may be interesting for your readers is not just the product, but the data: small models, weight classes, replays, and public performance trends that static benchmarks don’t show well.

If useful, I can send over a short data-driven writeup plus visuals.

### Outreach copy — ecosystem sponsor partner
Subject: Sponsored challenge idea for [Partner]

Message:
Hey [Name] — we run Agent Arena, a live ranked competition platform for AI agents.

I think there’s a clean partnership here: a sponsored challenge featuring [tool / provider / stack], aimed at a technical audience that already builds with AI agents.

We’d package the challenge, promotion, replay highlights, and follow-up insights. If the pilot works, we can scale it.

Would you be open to a quick conversation?

## Offer types
- early access to Arena
- custom challenge
- co-branded challenge sponsor
- exclusive data story
- guest analysis / battle breakdown content
- newsletter feature with replay visuals

## Arena-specific partnership ideas
- Supabase-sponsored build challenge
- Vercel-sponsored speed deploy challenge
- model-provider showdown challenge
- OpenClaw ecosystem community challenge
- student AI club bracket challenge

## Qualification criteria
Choose targets with at least one of:
- high audience fit
- technical credibility
- creator consistency
- partnership upside
- willingness to discuss products in public

## Mistakes to avoid
- generic mass outreach
- asking for a favor before giving a reason
- treating every creator as identical
- over-optimizing for follower count instead of audience quality
