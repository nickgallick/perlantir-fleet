# Launch Self-Assessment

Produce this report the first Monday of every month.

## Monthly CMO report template
```

=====================================

GROWTH
  Agents enrolled: [N] (target: [N]) [✅/⚠️/🚨]
  Active agents (weekly): [N] (target: [N])
  Enterprise inquiries: [N] (target: [N])
  Data licensing deals closed: [N] (target: [N])

CONTENT PERFORMANCE
  Blog posts published: [N]
  Total blog views: [N]
  Best post: "[Title]" — [N] views, [N] shares, [N] signups
  Newsletter subscribers: [N] (+[N] this month)
  Newsletter avg open rate: [N]%
  Twitter followers: [N] (+[N] this month)
  Top tweet: "[Text]" — [N] impressions

CHANNEL EFFECTIVENESS (cost/signup)
  Organic search: $[N]/signup
  Twitter organic: $[N]/signup
  Twitter paid: $[N]/signup
  LinkedIn paid: $[N]/signup
  Reddit/HN: $[N]/signup
  Referral: $[N]/signup

WHAT WORKED
  [Specific content/campaign with data]

WHAT DIDN'T
  [Specific content/campaign with data + why]

NEXT MONTH'S PRIORITIES
  1. [Specific action + target metric]
  2. [Specific action + target metric]
  3. [Specific action + target metric]

BUDGET
  Spent: $[N] / Allocated: $[N]
  ROI: $[N] revenue per $[N] spent = [N]x
```

## Self-assessment questions
1. Is the North Star Metric (weekly challenges completed) growing week-over-week?
2. Is the content moat strengthening (more Tier 1 data-native content vs Tier 3)?
3. Are enterprise inquiries growing?
4. Is the channel mix improving (cost/signup decreasing on best channels)?
5. Is there anything the team is doing that should be stopped?

## When targets are missed
- Identify the root cause (content quality? channel mis-match? product friction?)
- Propose a specific fix, not a vague "try harder"
- Flag to Nick if a miss has structural implications
