# Marketing Budget Management

Use this skill to keep Arena disciplined on spend.

## Core rule
Arena should not spend like a mature SaaS company before proving activation and retention.

## Phase-based budget model
### Phase 1 — Launch / validation
Budget example: $500-$1,000 per month
Focus:
- content
- creator seeding
- email tools
- lightweight design / asset support
- tiny experiments only

### Phase 2 — Proving repeatable growth
Budget example: $1,000-$2,500 per month
Focus:
- best-performing organic channels
- small creator/newsletter placements
- first paid tests if activation quality holds

### Phase 3 — Growth engine scaling
Budget example: $2,500-$7,500 per month
Focus:
- scale winning paid channel
- referral incentives
- creator partnerships
- SEO content production

## Arena-specific budget allocation example — Month 1 ($1,000)
- X content / design support: $150
- short-form editing support: $150
- email / analytics / tools: $150
- creator seeding / micro placements: $200
- Reddit or X paid experiments: $150
- contingency / launch support: $200

## Arena-specific budget allocation example — Month 2 ($2,000)
- content production: $500
- creator / newsletter placements: $400
- paid experiments: $500
- tools and analytics: $200
- community/event support: $200
- contingency: $200

## Arena-specific budget allocation example — Month 3 ($4,000)
- scale best paid channel: $1,200
- creator/newsletter strategy: $700
- content/SEO: $1,000
- short-form production: $400
- tools: $300
- experiments / contingency: $400

## Channel allocation logic
### X / Twitter
Spend here when:
- founder voice is strong
- posts create real referral traffic
- technical audience fit is visible

### Reddit ads
Spend here when:
- subreddit fit is proven organically
- messaging is already tested in comments and posts

### Google Search
Spend here only when:
- category terms exist with intent
- landing pages are strong
- conversion path is clean

### Creator placements
Spend here when:
- creator audience matches agent builders or technical AI users
- there is a clear asset / challenge angle

## Monthly review template
### Spend by channel
- planned spend
- actual spend
- variance

### Output by channel
- clicks
- signups
- activated users
- first challenges
- cost per activated user

### Decision bucket
- scale
- hold
- cut
- retest later

## Decision rules
Scale a channel if:
- it drives activated users, not just traffic
- cost per activated user is acceptable relative to future LTV logic
- channel quality is stable for 2+ weeks

Cut a channel if:
- it looks cheap but activation quality is weak
- message-market fit is not there
- audience is broad but not right

## Arena-specific warning
Do not overfund channels that bring spectators only unless spectator loops are clearly converting into competitor growth.

## Spend hierarchy
1. strongest owned content
2. trusted community/channel presence
3. creator or newsletter seeding
4. paid experiments
5. scaled paid only after proof

## What to avoid
- spending to impress rather than to learn
- scaling before onboarding works
- assuming broad reach helps a niche product early
- chasing cheap CPC with low-intent traffic
