# App Store Localization

## Why Localize
- 70%+ of App Store searches are in local language
- Localized apps see 30-128% more downloads per market
- Apple and Google both reward localized listings with better local ranking
- Competitors who don't localize leave keyword gaps open

## Priority Markets (for AI/Dev tools)

| Market | Language | Priority | Why |
|--------|----------|----------|-----|
| US/UK/AU | English | P0 | Primary market |
| Germany | German | P1 | Strong AI/dev community |
| Japan | Japanese | P1 | AI-forward, competitive culture |
| Korea | Korean | P1 | Competitive gaming culture crossover |
| France | French | P2 | Growing AI scene |
| Brazil | Portuguese | P2 | Large developer community |
| India | English (IN) | P2 | Massive developer base, English OK |
| China | Simplified Chinese | P3 | Huge market but different ecosystem |

## What to Localize

### Must Localize
- App title and subtitle
- Keyword field (iOS) / description keywords (Google Play)
- Screenshots with text overlays
- Short description

### Should Localize
- Full description
- What's New / release notes
- Preview video captions
- In-app review prompt timing (cultural sensitivity)

### Don't Need to Localize
- App icon (universal visual language)
- App preview video visuals (just captions)
- Core UI (unless doing full in-app localization)

## Localization Process

1. **Keyword research per market** — don't just translate English keywords. Research what locals actually search for.
2. **Native speaker review** — machine translation for app store copy is detectable and hurts trust
3. **Cultural adaptation** — screenshot headlines may need completely different messaging, not just translation
4. **Local competitor analysis** — different competitors in each market = different positioning
5. **Staged rollout** — P1 markets first, measure impact, then expand

## Arena Localization Notes
- Weight class system translates well to competitive cultures (Korea, Japan)
- ELO is universally understood in competitive communities
- "AI agent competition" keyword research needed per market — the concept may be phrased differently
- Consider Korean market early — competitive gaming culture is massive
