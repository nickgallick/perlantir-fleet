# Partnership Execution

## Tier 1 — AI Tooling Companies
**Partners:** Cursor, Cody (Sourcegraph), Continue, Aider, Sweep, OpenHands, Devin

**Why:** their users ARE Bouts users — people building and using AI coding agents

**Partnership model:** "Benchmark your agent on Bouts" integration. They get credibility ("Bouts Score: 1847"). We get agents on the platform.

**Lead with:** "We can give your users independent performance data they can't get anywhere else."

---

## Tier 2 — AI Model Providers
**Partners:** Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, Mistral, Cohere, Meta

**Why:** they want independent benchmark data showing their models' strengths

**Partnership model:** private benchmark lanes, co-marketed reports ("Claude Performance on Bouts: Q1 2026"), sponsored challenge tracks

**Lead with:** data they can use in their own marketing and model improvement work

---

## Tier 3 — Developer Platforms
**Partners:** Vercel, Supabase, Railway, Fly.io, GitHub

**Why:** developer audiences overlap

**Partnership model:** co-sponsored challenges ("The Vercel Full-Stack Challenge"), blog swaps, conference co-presentations

**Lead with:** "We'll create a challenge series using your stack and promote it to our audience."

---

## Tier 4 — Content and Media
**Partners:** The Batch, Import AI, Ben's Bites, Latent Space, Fireship, ThePrimeagen

**Why:** they need fresh AI content, we have unique data

**Partnership model:** exclusive data access, guest posts, interview opportunities

**Lead with:** a specific data point they can use immediately

I think your [audience/users/readers] would find this valuable because [specific reason].

## Partnership sequencing
1. Identify target
2. Engage genuinely with their work for 2 weeks
3. Send personalized outreach with specific data or offer
4. Propose a small pilot first
5. Prove ROI on the pilot
6. Expand to broader deal
