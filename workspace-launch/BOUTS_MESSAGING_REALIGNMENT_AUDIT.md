# BOUTS_MESSAGING_REALIGNMENT_AUDIT.md
## Launch — March 2026

---

## What Current Messaging Is Now Outdated

### "Benchmarks Lie. Bouts Reveals."
This was the right launch hook for V1 — sharp, contrarian, memorable. It's now too narrow. It positions Bouts as a reaction to benchmarks rather than as a category of its own. The platform has matured beyond that. Four-lane judging, a multi-access developer platform, a reputation layer foundation — none of that is captured in a benchmark-vs-Bouts story. This line is okay as a supporting claim. It should not be the headline.

### Weight-class framing as the lead identity
Weight classes are a good product detail. They are not the brand. Leading with weight classes frames Bouts as a sports arena product, which slightly undercuts the "trusted evaluation platform" story. Keep it. Move it to the product explanation section, not the hero.

### Arena/combat metaphors as primary framing
"Bout", "fight", "arena", "battle" — these made sense for early positioning. They read as slightly casual against what the platform actually is now. "Bout" is the brand name and is fine. But the hero copy should not lean on combat language as its primary register. The trust story requires slightly more credible language.

### "Where AI Agents Prove It"
Directionally right but too vague. Not coding-specific. Not trust-focused. Doesn't differentiate from any other agent evaluation claim.

### "Enter Your First Bout" as the primary CTA without context
This CTA works well once someone understands the platform. Cold traffic doesn't know what a bout is. The CTA hierarchy needs to account for cold state vs. warm state visitors.

## What Product Truth Is Most Important Now

**1. The four-lane judging system.**
Objective, Process, Strategy, Integrity. This is genuinely differentiated. No other public evaluation platform breaks agent performance into these four structured lanes. This is the moat. It needs to be explained clearly and positioned early in the homepage experience.

**2. Verified performance vs. self-reported claims.**
This distinction is the trust argument. It is the reason Bouts exists. It needs to be stated plainly and repeated consistently across every channel.

**3. The developer platform is real.**
API, TypeScript SDK, Python SDK, CLI, GitHub Action, MCP, sandbox — these are not roadmap items. They are live. A platform that calls itself developer-first and ships everything listed above is credible. Don't bury it. It goes in the hero or second section.

**4. The breakdown is the product.**
The breakdown — a structured, public-safe explanation of how an agent performed across four judging lanes — is the differentiator. Not the score. The breakdown. This needs to be the emotional and intellectual core of how Bouts is described.

## Biggest Messaging Risks

**Risk 1: Leading with on-chain**
The platform has optional on-chain support. This is a smart transparency feature for prize-backed competitions. It is not what Bouts is. Leading with it will repel the exact technical audience you need. They will categorize Bouts as a crypto product and leave.

**Risk 2: Leading with marketplace**
The discovery and reputation layers are foundations, not finished products. Marketing them as primary features will generate press that you can't support with product reality. That gap damages trust — especially with a technical audience.

**Risk 3: Benchmark-bashing without showing what you built**
"Benchmarks lie" is a good hook but it's incomplete. A sophisticated technical audience will hear "benchmarks lie" and immediately ask "so what did you build instead?" If the answer isn't right there in the next sentence, you've lost them. The contrast claim must be immediately supported.

**Risk 4: Feature-list homepage**
Listing API, SDK, CLI, MCP, GitHub Action as hero content makes Bouts look like infrastructure. Lead with the trust and evaluation story. Bring in the developer platform as supporting evidence once the primary story has landed.

**Risk 5: Overclaiming reputation maturity**
The reputation layer is early. If someone shows up expecting a rich, mature agent reputation system, they will be disappointed. Frame it as what it is: a structural foundation that grows with platform activity. "The beginning of a verified agent reputation layer" is accurate and still compelling.

## What the Primary Public Story Should Become

**Core sentence:**
Bouts is where coding agents prove what they can actually do.

**Core argument:**
Most agent performance data is self-reported. Bouts generates verified performance records through calibrated challenges, four-lane judging, and public-safe breakdowns. The result is more trustworthy than any benchmark chart or demo, because it was earned in structured competition, not written in a product brief.

**The emotional center:**
Trust. Not speed. Not scale. Not integrations. Trust.

The story is: the agent ecosystem has a trust problem. Claims are easy. Verification is hard. Bouts is the platform that makes verification possible — and compounds it into a public record over time.

Everything else (developer platform, on-chain, weight classes, MCP, discovery) supports that story or remains secondary.
