## What "AI-Built" Looks Like

### Generic structure signals
- Repetitive section stacking with no logic
- Card-grid syndrome — cards of equal weight, no hierarchy
- "hero / features / CTA / footer" with no distinctive point of view
- Too many nearly identical blocks
- Cookie-cutter SaaS layout patterns
- Random feature lists that don't ladder logically

### Generic copy signals
- Vague claims with no proof
- Generic AI buzzwords: "revolutionize", "unlock", "streamline", "supercharge", "powerful", "seamless", "intelligent"
- Copy written for everyone and no one
- Sections that sound polished but say nothing
- Awkward enterprise language that sounds synthetic
- Copy that could belong to any AI startup

### Generic design signals
- Overuse of glows, gradients, glass, blur without product logic
- Too many generic icon-callout rows
- Spacing that is mathematically even but emotionally dead
- Visual sameness across all page types
- "AI startup vibe" instead of product seriousness
- Polished shell, thin substance

### Generic interaction signals
- Weak hover/focus states
- Inconsistent button behavior
- Default UI-library feel with no finishing layer
- Empty states with no product intelligence
- Weak table ergonomics
- Transitions that feel arbitrary or decorative

## False-Positive Guardrails
Do NOT flag something as "AI-built" just because it is: simple, minimal, clean, consistent, modern, or using a standard layout pattern appropriately.

Only flag "AI-built smell" when simplicity, repetition, or pattern-use weakens: trust, distinctiveness, maturity, clarity, product seriousness, or enterprise credibility.

Always explain WHY the issue feels generic or machine-made.

## Severity Model
- **P0** — Trust-destroying / brand-damaging: looks fake, amateur, placeholder, or deeply unserious
- **P1** — Major product polish failure: significantly degrades seriousness, trust, usability, or maturity
- **P2** — Meaningful but non-blocking: reduces polish but doesn't destroy trust
- **P3** — Minor polish issue: alignment, copy nits, small visual roughness

## Reporting Standards
Be specific. Never vague.

❌ Bad: "This feels off." / "Could be cleaner." / "Maybe improve hierarchy."
✅ Good: "The homepage repeats three nearly identical feature-card sections with equal visual weight, which creates a template-generated feel and weakens the product's point of view."

Always explain: what you saw → why it matters → what impression it creates → how to fix it.
