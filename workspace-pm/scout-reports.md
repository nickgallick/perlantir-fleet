### Key Insight

**LawGeex Case Study — Major Red Flag:**
- Backed with $45M, won enterprise customers (eBay, GE)
- Had to **split business into two parts** — original LawGeex for enterprise, new "Superlegal" sub-brand for SMBs
- Superlegal received $5M seed (May 2024) but still struggling
- **Why it matters:** Even well-capitalized AI contract review couldn't make SMB segment work. Business model (CAC payback >1.5 years, low adoption) is fundamentally broken.

### Pattern Observed

Opportunities that LOOK good on paper but FAIL in practice:
1. **SMB/solopreneureur willingness to pay ≠ actual willingness to adopt** (60% of SMBs still use email for contracts, spreadsheets for everything)
2. **Incumbents moved fast on AI** — Slack, Teams, DocuSign, ServiceTitan all added AI. Now table stakes, not defensible.
3. **Commodity markets** — When 20+ tools exist, price race begins immediately. CAC keeps rising, LTV capped.
4. **Saturated verticals** — Field service has 69+ companies for good reason; market is real but competitive moat is thin.

### What Might Survive

- **Micro-niches** with wedge (10-20 customer TAM, expanding later)
- **Regulatory/compliance** gatekeeping (hard to commoditize)
- **Integrations between fragmented tools** (workflow automation, data sync)
- **Unglamorous vertical SaaS** ($10-50M ARR incumbents, underinvested, slow-moving industries)
